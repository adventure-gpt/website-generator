Hey {{USER_NAME}}! {{ADMIN_NAME}} set this up so you can make your own websites just by describing what you want. Here's how it works.

## The Short Version

1. **Open {{EDITOR_NAME}}** (the app on your desktop)
2. **Open the AI chat** in the sidebar
3. **Tell it what you want** in plain English
4. **Look at it in your browser** — it'll open it for you or tell you where to look
5. **Ask for changes** until you're happy
6. **Say "deploy"** and it goes live on a real website URL anyone can visit

That's it. The AI handles all the technical stuff.

## What To Say to the AI

Talk to it like a person. Here are some examples:

**Making something new:**
- "Make me a website to track my daily workouts"
- "I want a pretty page with my favorite recipes"
- "Create a birthday invitation page for Mom's party"

**Changing something:**
- "Make the colors warmer"
- "Add a section for photos"
- "The button isn't working"
- "I don't like it, try something completely different"

**Putting it online:**
- "Deploy this"
- "Put it online"
- "I want to share this with people"

**Updating a live site:**
- "Push the changes" or "Update the live site"

**Other:**
- "Show me my projects"
- "Open the recipe site locally"
- "Delete the test page project"

You don't need special words or commands. Just describe what you want.

## Where Your Stuff Lives

- **Your projects** are in the `projects` folder inside your workspace. Each website gets its own folder in there. You don't need to touch these — the AI manages them.
- **Desktop shortcuts** — when you deploy a site, the AI creates a shortcut on your desktop. Double-click to open your live site.
- **Live URLs** — every deployed site gets a URL like `your-project.pages.dev` that works on any device.
- **Your account** — every site has its own login. You'll create an account (email + password) the first time you use each site. After that, your data is private and synced — log in on your phone and you see the same stuff as on your computer.

## Using Sites on Your Phone

Every site you make can be added to your phone's home screen so it works like a regular app. Here's how:

**iPhone (Safari):**
1. Open the site in Safari
2. Tap the share button (the square with an arrow)
3. Scroll down and tap "Add to Home Screen"
4. Tap "Add"

**Android (Chrome):**
1. Open the site in Chrome
2. Tap the three-dot menu
3. Tap "Add to Home Screen" or "Install App"
4. Tap "Add"

After that, it shows up as an icon on your home screen and opens fullscreen like a real app. It even works if you lose internet for a bit.

## Local Preview vs. Live

When the AI builds something, it runs a local preview in your browser. This is only visible to you, on your computer. It updates instantly when the AI makes changes.

When you say "deploy," the AI uploads it to the internet. After that, changes you make locally won't show up on the live site until you tell the AI to deploy again.

**Make changes → preview locally → happy? → deploy**

## If Something Goes Wrong

Just describe what's wrong:
- "The page is blank"
- "It looks weird on my phone"
- "I'm getting an error"
- "Nothing happens when I click the button"

The AI will work through it. If it takes a while, that's normal — it's trying different approaches to fix things. It'll keep you posted with quick updates like "still working on it." It's very persistent and can solve almost anything on its own, so just let it cook.

---

## Tips

- **Be specific when you can.** "Make the background a soft pink" works better than "make it prettier" (though both work — the AI will just make a judgment call on vague requests).
- **It's okay to say no.** If the AI makes something you don't like, say so. "Try again," "do something completely different," or "go back to how it was" all work.
- **Each website is separate.** If you want to change your recipe site, mention it by name so the AI knows which one. If you only have one project, it'll figure it out.
- **Nothing breaks permanently.** The AI tracks versions, so if something gets messed up, it can undo changes and try again.
